Special Diet

Boiled Chicken

Boiled chicken, a fuss-free protein, is both simple and health-conscious. Quick to prepare, it stays tender while preserving its natural taste. This lean option fits effortlessly into diverse meals, from salads to wraps, making it a versatile kitchen essential. Boiled chicken's ease of preparation and compatibility with different flavors make it an ideal choice for those looking for a straightforward, nutritious addition to their meals.

Bread Salad

Bread salad, a delightful and inventive dish, combines the comfort of fresh bread with the vibrancy of a garden-fresh salad. Crispy cubes of toasted bread add a delightful crunch to a medley of colorful vegetables, creating a harmonious blend of textures. Tossed with a zesty vinaigrette or a light dressing, this salad transforms day-old bread into a tasty centerpiece, offering a perfect balance of flavors. Whether featuring ripe tomatoes, crisp cucumbers, or other seasonal favorites, a bread salad is a refreshing and satisfying choice for those seeking a unique twist on a classic favorite.

Dry Fruits

Dried fruits, nature's sweet treasures, are nutrient-packed snacks that offer a convenient burst of energy and a satisfying natural sweetness. Whether it's the chewy richness of raisins, the intense flavor of dried apricots, or the exotic touch of dried mango, these portable delights provide a concentrated source of vitamins, minerals, and fiber. Packed with antioxidants, dried fruits are not only a delicious treat but also contribute to overall well-being. Whether enjoyed on their own as a snack, added to cereals, or mixed into salads, dried fruits offer a wholesome and versatile way to indulge in the goodness of nature's bounty.

Healthy Fruits

A variety of fruits stand out as nutritional powerhouses, offering a delicious way to boost overall health. Berries, such as blueberries, strawberries, and raspberries, are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and fiber, promoting heart health and aiding in digestion. Apples, with their natural sweetness and fiber content, contribute to stable blood sugar levels and support digestive health. Avocado, while technically a fruit, is an excellent source of healthy monounsaturated fats, promoting heart health and providing a creamy texture to dishes. Bananas offer a quick energy boost due to their natural sugars and are also a good source of potassium. Citrus fruits like oranges, grapefruits, and lemons are packed with vitamin C, supporting the immune system. Including a variety of these fruits in your diet ensures a well-rounded intake of essential nutrients for a vibrant and healthy lifestyle.

Fresh Juices

Fresh juices, a vibrant symphony of flavors and nutrients, provide a refreshing way to hydrate and nourish the body. Whether it's the zesty kick of citrus blends or the earthy sweetness of vegetable concoctions, freshly squeezed juices offer a natural burst of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. From classic orange and apple juices to trendy green juices featuring kale and spinach, these beverages contribute to overall well-being and hydration. Customizable and versatile, fresh juices can be tailored to individual taste preferences and dietary needs. Whether enjoyed as a morning pick-me-up or a revitalizing afternoon treat, incorporating a variety of fresh juices into your routine adds a delicious and healthful dimension to your hydration regimen.

Some Proteins

Protein, an essential macronutrient, comes in various delicious and nutritious forms. Lean meats like chicken and turkey offer a high-quality source of animal protein, supporting muscle health and providing essential amino acids. Fish, such as salmon and tuna, not only supply protein but also deliver omega-3 fatty acids for heart and brain health. Plant-based options like lentils, beans, and quinoa are excellent choices for those seeking protein from vegetarian sources, offering fiber and a range of vitamins and minerals. Greek yogurt and cottage cheese are versatile dairy options that pack a protein punch, perfect for snacks or breakfast. Nuts and seeds, like almonds and chia seeds, are not only convenient but also rich in protein, healthy fats, and other essential nutrients. Incorporating a variety of these protein sources into your diet ensures a well-rounded and delicious approach to meeting your nutritional need

What Is A Healthy Weight Range ?


Your Body Mass Index (BMI) determines your healthy body weight range.


BMI falls under these categories –


Below 18.5 - underweight
18.5 – 24.9 - healthy or normal
25 – 29.9 - overweight
Above 30 - obese
